Recipe: Herb Jam

A tasty way to eliminate food waste!

A common wasteful practice in households across the country is the act of tossing the tops and stems of herbs and vegetables in the trash or compost. These gems are not only edible, but they are also loaded with flavor and nutrients!

For instance; carrots, beets, radish, fennel tops, stems of cilantro, parsley, dill, and garlic scape can all come together in a beautiful medley of flavor when simmered in garlicky olive oil! Once cooled, blend them up and enjoy as a savory spread on bread or crackers. You can even add a dollop to a soup or stew, or swirl into hummus for a lively appetizer!

Let's dive into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch carrot tops
  • 1 bunch fennel tops
  • 1 bunch dill
  • 1 bunch (about 8) garlic scapes
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup olive oil (or more if necessary)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Got all the ingredients? Great! It's time to start cooking!

Preparation

  1. Steam the tops, herbs, and garlic until tender (about 20 minutes).
  2. Let it cool, then remove the greens and garlic.
  3. Separate the garlic and mash with a fork until creamy.
  4. Heat oil over medium and add garlic (do not allow it to burn or the garlic will become too bitter).
  5. Chop the greens and add them to the oil and garlic mixture.
  6. Cook at a low simmer for about 45 minutes, frequently stirring and mashing with a wooden spoon.
  7. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground pepper.
  8. Remove heat and let cool to room temperature.
  9. Transfer to a food processor and blend until your desired consistency.

If you choose not to eat immediately, you can refrigerate in a tightly sealed container, or freeze in jars. Make sure to bring it back to room temperature before serving!
